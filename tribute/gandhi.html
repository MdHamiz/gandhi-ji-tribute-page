Mahatma Gandhi

A Tribute to the Father of the Nation

Mahatma Gandhi, born Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, was one of the most influential figures in India’s struggle for independence. Known worldwide for his philosophy of non-violent resistance, or satyagraha, Gandhi inspired movements for civil rights and freedom across the globe.

Biography

Born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, India, Mahatma Gandhi studied law in London and began his career as an advocate in South Africa, where he first employed his philosophy of non-violent resistance to combat racial discrimination. Upon returning to India, Gandhi emerged as a leader in the fight for India’s independence from British rule.

Throughout his life, Gandhi’s commitment to truth, simplicity, and justice led him to dedicate his life to social causes, including the upliftment of the poor and the struggle for civil rights. His teachings continue to inspire millions today.

Key Achievements

  • Non-Cooperation Movement (1920-1922): Gandhi encouraged Indians to boycott British goods and institutions to promote self-reliance and weaken British rule.
  • Dandi Salt March (1930): To protest the British monopoly on salt, Gandhi led a 240-mile march to the Arabian Sea to make salt from seawater, symbolizing civil disobedience.
  • Quit India Movement (1942): Gandhi called for an end to British rule in India, which led to mass protests and paved the way for India's independence in 1947.

Famous Quotes

“Be the change that you wish to see in the world.”
“The best way to find yourself is to lose yourself in the service of others.”
“In a gentle way, you can shake the world.”
